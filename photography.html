Behind the Lens

The Other
Experiment

Photography, like semiconductor research, is fundamentally about controlling light. Whether characterizing RRAM device behavior under pulsed light conditions in the lab or chasing a fleeting dusk across the Hudson Valley, both disciplines demand patience, precision, and the ability to find signal in noise.

I photograph the landscapes of upstate New York, the streets of Bangladesh, and the abstract geometries of the lab — searching for the moments where science and beauty briefly coincide.
